What is Dental Implants?

Implants are generally considered the best solution when it comes to replacing lost teeth.

Dental implants are a durable and natural-looking solution for missing or damaged teeth. They involve placing a small, biocompatible post into the jawbone, which serves as a replacement tooth root. A custom-made dental crown is then attached to the post, providing a strong and natural-looking replacement tooth. At Dental Scotland, we offer a variety of types of dental implants to meet the unique needs of our patients.

How does Invisalign works?

When you come in for an Invisalign® treatment plan at Dental Scotland practices, there are two stages – a free consultation and treatment:

Consultation

Your dentist will first assess your teeth to establish if an Invisalign® teeth straightening treatment is right for you. We take a high-tech computerised 3D image of your mouth and teeth. We will be able to show you a simulated evolution and timeline of your straightening plan together with the anticipated end result.

Treatment

Every two weeks, you receive a new set of Invisalign® clear aligners to wear over your teeth all day except for when you are eating. Wearing the aligners progressively moves your teeth to the desired position. Every 4 – 6 weeks we will plan an appointment to review the progress of your treatment at the practice. Depending on your individual needs, your Invisalign® treatment may last from 3 to 18 months. As only gentle pressure is required to move or straighten teeth, the majority of patients experience no more than slight discomfort during the treatment. At the end of your treatment, a retention appliance may be required in order to prevent teeth from returning to their original position.
